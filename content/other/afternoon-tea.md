### Afternoon Tea

Afternoon sun filtered through swaying, sea-blue whispers of melamine beads and aged linen. Every day, from late morning to evening, golden light drenched the space between these curtains, steeping its dedicated resident in daylong warmth.

Ms. Snickers was sprawled comfortably over the periwinkle table runner, seeming to melt into the table as sunlight spread like sweetened malt through her whole being. Her whiskered, tan face was tucked neatly behind two curled paws. Her only movements were the gentle tremble of her pale, generous sides as she purred, and the occasional flick of her brown-tufted ears scattering the steam from a nearby teacup.

The cup's gilded rim, slightly chipped, matched the little bell nestled in the wispy fur of Ms. Snickers' chest. On the teacup’s white surface, two Siamese kittens danced paw-in-paw, dressed in tutus matching their baby blue eyes. The kittens rose when gentle fingers lifted the teacup, halos aglimmer as they twirled, then landed with a quiet porcelain click. 

Sleepily, Ms. Snickers revealed her gaze—a soft, hazy myriad of colors through the steam—before she closed her eyes once more, to dream.
